## Why Precision Matters in Legal Document Translation

Legal translations are not just about converting words from one language to another—they are about preserving intent, safeguarding compliance, and protecting the interests of all parties involved. A single misinterpretation in international contracts or an error in a notarial deed can carry significant legal and financial consequences. That’s why the translation process for official documents demands expertise and a meticulous approach.

### The Role of Sworn and Certified Legal Translators

Sworn translations are required for many legal documents in Belgium, especially when these documents are to be used in court or submitted to government agencies. Certified legal translators, recognized by the appropriate authorities, guarantee that translations are accurate and legally binding. These professionals understand both the source and target legal systems, reducing the risk of misinterpretations in international contracts and ensuring that every translated document holds up under scrutiny.

### Risks of Mistranslations in Legal Contexts

- **Legal risks:** An inaccurate translation can lead to disputes, contract nullification, or even litigation.
- **Compliance issues:** Mistakes in compliance-related documents can result in regulatory penalties or delays.
- **Reputational harm:** Trust and reliability are essential in legal matters; a single error can damage professional relationships.

A translation agency specialising in legal translation services can implement rigorous proofreading and review processes, as well as error correction protocols, to eliminate these risks.
