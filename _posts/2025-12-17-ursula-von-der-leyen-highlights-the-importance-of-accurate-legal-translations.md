## Why Accuracy in Legal Translations Matters

Legal documents—from international contracts to notarial deeds—carry weighty consequences. A single misinterpretation can expose organizations to legal risks, compliance breaches, or financial loss. High-stakes cases involving cross-border agreements demonstrate that accuracy is not just a preference; it is a legal necessity. Ursula von der Leyen’s leadership on the European stage reminds us that clear legal communication supports fair, transparent, and enforceable agreements across languages and jurisdictions.

**Key reasons accuracy is crucial:**

- **Upholding Contracts:** International contracts require precise legal translations to ensure all parties share the same understanding.
- **Court Proceedings:** Sworn translations and certified legal translators guarantee that every word stands up in court.
- **Regulatory Compliance:** Misinterpretations in official documents can lead to compliance issues, delays, or penalties.

## The Role of Sworn and Certified Translators

Sworn translators and certified legal translators play a pivotal role in delivering trustworthy legal translations. Their expertise ensures that translations are legally valid for use in courts, public offices, and with notaries throughout Belgium and the EU. Certified translations are often required for:

- Legalizations & apostilles
- Immigration documents
- Notarial deeds
- Corporate filings and compliance records

By working with a specialized translation agency, you benefit from error correction, proofreading and review processes that minimize legal risks and uphold the integrity of your documents.

## Risks of Mistranslations in Legal Contexts

Even minor errors in legal document translation can have significant consequences. Misinterpretations in international contracts may invalidate agreements, cause disputes, or result in financial loss. Incorrect translations of official documents can delay or derail legal proceedings. The value of quality over speed cannot be overstated; a rushed translation could compromise your legal position.
