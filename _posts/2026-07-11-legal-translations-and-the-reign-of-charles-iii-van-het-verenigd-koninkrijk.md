## Why Accuracy Is Non-Negotiable in Legal Translations

Legal translations are not just about language—they are about law. A single misinterpretation in an international contract or a notarial deed can have far-reaching consequences. For instance:

- **Misinterpretations in international contracts** may lead to costly disputes or even invalidate agreements.
- **Errors in official documents** can delay compliance processes or lead to legal risks for businesses and public bodies.
- **Inaccuracies in legalizations & apostilles** can result in documents being rejected by foreign authorities.

This is why sworn translations and certified legal translators are essential. These specialists ensure every word holds its legal weight in multilingual communication, reducing the risk of costly or reputation-damaging errors.

## The Role of Sworn and Certified Legal Translators

Sworn translators are authorised to provide translations that are legally valid and recognised by courts and government agencies. In Belgium, a sworn translation guarantees that the translated document is an accurate and faithful representation of the original—critical when dealing with sensitive information such as contracts, immigration papers, or compliance documentation.

Certified legal translators bring an additional layer of trust and reliability. Their expertise ensures that:

- Legal terminology is accurately conveyed across languages
- Cultural and jurisdictional nuances are respected
- Translations are fit for purpose, whether for litigation, contract negotiations, or regulatory filings

## The Risks of Mistranslations in Legal Contexts

Legal translations must prioritise quality over speed. Even minor errors can introduce legal risks:

- **Ambiguity in notarial deeds** can undermine property transfers or estate settlements
- **Faulty translation of compliance requirements** may result in regulatory breaches
- **Inaccurate translation strategy** could lead to reputational damage for law firms or corporations
