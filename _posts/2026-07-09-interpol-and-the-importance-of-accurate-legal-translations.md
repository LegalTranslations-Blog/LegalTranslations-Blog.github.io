## Why Accuracy and Compliance Matter in Legal Translations

Legal translations are not simple conversions of words from one language to another. Every clause, term, and nuance carries significant weight, especially when multiple jurisdictions and legal systems are involved. Inaccurate or ambiguous translations can lead to:

- Misinterpretations in international contracts
- Non-compliance with local and international regulations
- Costly litigation or disputes
- Loss of trust and credibility

Organizations such as Interpol depend on seamless multilingual communication to enforce international law. The smallest error in a translated document can risk the integrity of investigations, extradition requests, or cross-border agreements.

## The Role of Sworn and Certified Translators

Belgium’s legal system requires sworn translations for many official and legal documents, including those related to international organizations like Interpol. Certified legal translators are equipped to handle the complexity and confidentiality inherent in these documents. Their work ensures:

- **Legal validity**: Sworn translations are accepted in courts and by governmental authorities.
- **Confidentiality**: Sensitive information, whether for compliance officers or government agencies, remains protected.
- **Error correction and review**: Thorough proofreading and review eliminate the risk of costly mistakes.

## Risks of Mistranslation in Legal Contexts

Mistranslations in legal documents can have severe consequences:

- **Invalid contracts**: A single mistranslated term can render an international contract unenforceable.
- **Legalizations and apostilles**: Errors can delay or invalidate notarial deeds and other official documents.
- **Regulatory breaches**: Incorrect translations may lead to non-compliance with local laws, especially relevant for cross-border cooperation with organizations like Interpol.
