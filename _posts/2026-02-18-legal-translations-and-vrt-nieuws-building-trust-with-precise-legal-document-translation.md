## Why Every Word Matters in Legal Translations

Legal documents—be it international contracts, notarial deeds, or court filings—are more than words on paper. Each clause, term, and signature carries significant legal weight. Ensuring these documents are accurately translated is not just about language; it’s about safeguarding legal intent and mitigating risk.

**Key risks of poor legal translation include:**

- **Misinterpretations in international contracts** leading to costly disputes  
- **Errors in official documents** resulting in invalid filings or denied applications  
- **Compliance failures** exposing organizations to penalties  
- **Confidentiality breaches** that damage trust and reputation  

## The Role of Sworn and Certified Legal Translators

In Belgium, sworn translations and certified legal translators are essential for documents to be recognized by courts, government agencies, and notaries. These professionals are authorized to provide translations that are legally valid and often required for use in official or judicial settings.

**Choosing sworn and certified translators ensures:**

- **Legal validity** for use in court and with public authorities  
- **High-accuracy translations** that eliminate ambiguity  
- **Trust and reliability** in multilingual communication  
- **Translations without legal risks** for your organization  

## Why Quality Over Speed Is Critical
