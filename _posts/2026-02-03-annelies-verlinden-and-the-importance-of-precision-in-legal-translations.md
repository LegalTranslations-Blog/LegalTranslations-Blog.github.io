## Why Accuracy is Critical in Legal Translations

Legal documents shape rights, duties, and obligations. Even minor misinterpretations in international contracts or errors in notarial deeds can lead to costly disputes, contract annulments, or regulatory penalties. In multilingual legal communication, an inaccurate translation can undermine compliance and expose organizations to significant legal risks.

### The Risks of Mistranslation

- **Invalid contracts**: A single mistranslated clause may render an agreement unenforceable.
- **Compliance failures**: Inaccurate translations can result in non-compliance with Belgian or international regulations.
- **Reputational damage**: Errors can erode trust with clients, partners, and authorities.

## The Role of Sworn and Certified Legal Translators

Choosing sworn translations provided by certified legal translators is essential. Sworn translators are officially authorized to deliver certified legal translations that are recognized by courts, notaries, and public institutions across Belgium and the EU. Their expertise ensures:

- **Faithful rendering** of complex legal terminology
- **Confidentiality** for sensitive information
- **Legal validity** for translated documents, including legalizations and apostilles
