## Why Accuracy in Legal Translations Matters in Belgium

Legal documents are binding. A single misinterpretation in an international contract or a notarial deed can have far-reaching consequences—ranging from compliance issues to costly litigation. In the context of the Baloise Belgium Tour 2026, organizations, sponsors, and participants may need to navigate multilingual communication, contracts, and regulatory requirements across Belgium’s linguistic regions.

### Key Risks of Mistranslations in International Contracts and Official Documents

- **Legal liability:** An error in translation may expose your organization to legal and financial risks.
- **Compliance failures:** Inaccurate translations can result in non-compliance with Belgian or EU regulations.
- **Contractual disputes:** Misinterpretations in international contracts may lead to disagreements or invalid agreements.
- **Delays:** Incorrect documentation can delay processes such as legalizations, apostilles, or official recognitions.

## The Role of Sworn and Certified Legal Translators in Belgium

Sworn translations and certified legal translators are fundamental in safeguarding the validity of legal documents. In Belgium, certain documents—such as court documents and notarial deeds—require sworn translations to be recognized by authorities. Certified legal translators are trained to maintain the integrity and confidentiality of sensitive information, ensuring that every word holds up in court or before notaries.

### When Are Sworn Translations Needed for Legal Documents?

- **Legalizations & apostilles** for international use
- **Official documents** for government agencies
- **Proofreading and review** to guarantee error correction and eliminate misinterpretations
