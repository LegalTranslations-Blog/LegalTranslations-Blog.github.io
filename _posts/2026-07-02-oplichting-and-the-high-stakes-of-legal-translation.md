## Why Accuracy Is Non-Negotiable in Belgian Legal Translations

Legal documents—whether contracts, agreements, or court submissions—leave no room for error. In oplichting cases, even a minor misinterpretation can tip the scales of justice. Accurate legal document translation preserves the exact nuances of the original text, ensuring that fraudulent clauses or loopholes do not go unnoticed due to linguistic ambiguity.

### The Risks of Misinterpretations in International Contracts and Official Documents

- **Unintended liabilities:** A faulty translation could introduce obligations not present in the original.
- **Contractual loopholes:** Errors may allow fraudulent parties to exploit mistranslations.
- **Invalid legal proceedings:** Courts in Belgium and abroad may reject documents that are not properly translated or certified.
- **Financial losses:** Misinterpretations in international contracts can lead to costly disputes and compliance failures.

For businesses and law firms, these risks highlight the importance of translations without legal risks.

## The Role of Sworn and Certified Legal Translators in Belgium

Not all translators are equipped to handle the complexity of legal texts. Sworn translations, provided by certified legal translators, are often required for official documents, legalizations, and apostilles in Belgium. These professionals have undergone rigorous vetting and are authorized to produce translations that are legally recognized by courts and public authorities.

Certified legal translators:

- Guarantee confidentiality and data protection, essential in oplichting cases.
- Provide error correction and thorough proofreading and review processes.
- Understand the legal systems and terminology in both the source and target languages.
- Ensure that notarial deeds, contracts, and other official documents meet all regulatory requirements.
