For entities such as STIB, which operates within a complex regulatory environment and serves a diverse population, having certified legal translators ensures that contracts, policies, and communication are accurately conveyed in all relevant languages.

**Key risks of mistranslation include:**
- Ambiguities in contract terms leading to disputes
- Unintentional breach of compliance obligations
- Loss of trust and credibility with stakeholders
- Costly legal proceedings due to preventable errors

## The Critical Role of Sworn and Certified Translators for Official Documents

Sworn translations are often required for official documents such as court filings, legalizations & apostilles, or cross-border agreements. Only sworn or certified legal translators—experts recognized by judicial authorities—can provide translations that are legally valid and recognized by public institutions in Belgium and abroad.

This is particularly relevant for STIB, where documentation may need to be submitted to government agencies or international partners. The involvement of certified legal translators is not just a formality; it is a safeguard against error correction issues and ensures every document holds up under legal scrutiny.

## Compliance and Confidentiality in Legal Document Translation in Belgium

Legal documents often contain sensitive information. A trustworthy translation agency follows strict confidentiality protocols to protect client data. Compliance with Belgian and EU data protection laws is non-negotiable, especially when handling personal information in contracts, notarial deeds, or corporate policies.
