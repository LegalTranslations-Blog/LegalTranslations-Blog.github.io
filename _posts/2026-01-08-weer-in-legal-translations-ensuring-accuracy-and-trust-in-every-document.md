For law firms, corporate legal departments, notaries, and public institutions, a single mistranslation can lead to:

- Invalid contracts
- Regulatory breaches
- Legal risks and liabilities
- Damage to professional reputation

## The Critical Role of Sworn and Certified Translators in Belgium

Sworn translators are officially authorized to deliver translations that hold legal value in Belgium. Their signature and stamp provide assurance that the translation is accurate and faithful to the original. Certified legal translators possess specialized training, enabling them to handle complex terminology in areas such as:

- International contracts
- Legalizations & apostilles
- Notarial deeds
- Official documents for court or compliance

By employing only sworn and certified professionals, agencies like Legal Translations guarantee translations that are valid and recognized by Belgian authorities and institutions.

## The Risks of Mistranslations in Legal Contexts

Mistranslations in legal documents can have far-reaching consequences:

- **Contractual disputes**: Ambiguities or errors can change the meaning of clauses, leading to disagreements or unenforceable contracts.
- **Compliance failures**: Inaccurate translation may result in non-compliance with Belgian or international regulations.
- **Reputational harm**: Misinterpretations in international contracts can undermine trust between parties.

Legal Translations specializes in *error correction* and *proofreading and review*, minimizing the risk of misinterpretations and ensuring every translation is legally sound.
