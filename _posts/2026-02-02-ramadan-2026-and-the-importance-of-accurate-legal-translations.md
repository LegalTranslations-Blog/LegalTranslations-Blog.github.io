## Why Accuracy in Legal Translations Matters During Ramadan 2026 in Belgium

Legal documents, whether they are international contracts, notarial deeds, or official correspondence, must be translated with precision. Even a minor error or misinterpretation can lead to significant legal risks, disputes, or delays in compliance. During Ramadan 2026, when many organizations in Belgium are handling sensitive negotiations and cross-border agreements, the stakes are even higher.

**Key reasons why accuracy is crucial:**

- **Legal Validity:** Sworn translations and certified legal translators ensure that translated documents are legally recognized in courts and by government agencies in Belgium.
- **Multilingual Communication:** Misinterpretations in international contracts or notarial deeds can cause misunderstandings that harm business relationships or result in non-compliance.
- **Compliance and Confidentiality:** Accurate legal document translation protects sensitive information and upholds the integrity of confidential agreements, particularly when legalizations and apostilles are involved.

## The Role of Sworn and Certified Translators in Belgium

Sworn translators are authorized professionals who provide certified translations of official and legal documents. In Belgium, their services are essential for legalizations, apostilles, and submissions to public institutions. Certified legal translators guarantee that every word is faithfully rendered, reducing the risk of costly errors and ensuring that translations hold up in court.

**Their responsibilities include:**

- Verifying the accuracy of translations
- Affixing official seals and signatures
- Providing error correction and review for maximum reliability

## Risks of Mistranslations in Legal Contexts in Belgium

A mistranslation in an international contract or notarial deed can have far-reaching consequences for Belgian law firms and businesses:

- **Legal disputes:** Ambiguous or incorrect wording can spark litigation or regulatory penalties.
- **Financial loss:** Miscommunication may void agreements or trigger compensation claims.
- **Compliance failure:** Inaccurate translations may result in rejected filings or non-compliance with Belgian and international regulations.
