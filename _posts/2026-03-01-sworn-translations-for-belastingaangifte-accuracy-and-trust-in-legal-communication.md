- **Trust and reliability:** Clear, accurate translations foster trust with clients, partners, and institutions in Belgium and beyond.
- **Translations without legal risks:** Correct translation prevents misunderstandings that may trigger audits or legal challenges within Belgian jurisdiction.

## The Role of Sworn and Certified Translators in Belgium’s Legal Translation Services

**Sworn translations** are not just a formality—they are a legal requirement for many tax and official filings in Belgium. Sworn translators are officially recognized and their work is accepted by courts, notaries, and government agencies across Belgium. Their expertise includes:

- **Certified legal translators** who understand tax terminology in multiple languages relevant for Belgian legal contexts
- Ensuring every translation is legally valid, recognized, and compliant with Belgian and EU standards
- Providing **legalizations & apostilles** when necessary for cross-border filings involving Belgian authorities

## Risks of Mistranslations in Belgian Legal Contexts

The consequences of errors or ambiguities in legal document translation within Belgium are far-reaching:

- **Misinterpretations in international contracts** can alter the meaning of tax liabilities or obligations in Belgian legal proceedings.
- Incorrect translations may invalidate documents submitted to Belgian tax authorities, leading to penalties or delays.
- Inadequate **proofreading and review** processes increase the risk of costly mistakes in Belgian legal communications.
