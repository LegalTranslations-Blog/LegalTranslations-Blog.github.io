## Why Accuracy in Legal Translations Matters

A single misinterpretation in an international contract or notarial deed can have significant consequences—invalidating agreements, incurring legal costs, or even jeopardizing entire business ventures. For law firms, corporate legal departments, and public institutions in Belgium, accuracy in legal translations is not just a preference; it is a requirement for compliance and protection against legal risks.

### Key Risks of Mistranslations

- **Misinterpretations in international contracts:** Small errors may alter obligations, leading to disputes.
- **Invalid official documents:** Inaccurate translations can render notarial deeds or other official documents unenforceable.
- **Compliance failures:** Regulatory requirements might not be met if translations are not precise and certified.

## The Role of Sworn and Certified Legal Translators

Belgian law mandates that certain legal documents, such as court submissions, official documents, and legalizations & apostilles, be translated by sworn translators. These professionals are authorized to provide certified translations, ensuring the target document holds the same legal weight as the original.

### Why Choose Certified Legal Translators?

- **Trust & reliability:** Sworn translations are recognized by courts, notaries, and public authorities.
- **Error correction and review:** Certified translators follow stringent proofreading and review protocols to eliminate mistakes.
- **Confidentiality:** Handling sensitive legal information requires a trusted partner who guarantees secure, confidential multilingual communication.
