## Why Sworn and Certified Legal Translators Matter for Belgian Legal Documents

When it comes to legal translation services, not all translators are created equal. Sworn translations—produced by certified legal translators—are often required for legalizations, apostilles, and submission to courts or notaries in Belgium. These professionals are authorized to provide translations that are recognized as legally valid and binding, ensuring:

- **Compliance** with Belgian and international regulations
- **Trust and reliability** in critical documentation
- **Translations without legal risks** arising from linguistic errors

## The Hidden Dangers of Misinterpretations in International Contracts and Official Documents

The incident aéroport Bruxelles is a powerful reminder of how misinterpretations in international contracts or official documents can have far-reaching consequences. Common pitfalls include:

- **Ambiguous terminology** leading to conflicting interpretations
- **Omissions or mistranslations** that can invalidate agreements
- **Errors in notarial deeds** causing delays or legal disputes

That’s why rigorous proofreading and review processes are essential to catch any discrepancies before documents are finalized.
