## Why Accuracy Matters in Legal Translations

Legal translations demand more than just linguistic fluency. Each term, clause, and phrase carries legal weight. A minor misinterpretation can lead to costly disputes, contract failures, or compliance breaches. For Belgian law firms, corporate legal departments, and public institutions, ensuring that every word in a legal document is translated with absolute accuracy is critical. This is particularly true when handling multilingual communication after incidents with international implications, such as the Eurostar ongeval.

**Key risks of errors in legal translations:**

- **Misinterpretations in international contracts:** Even a single word out of place can change the meaning of a contract, exposing your organization to legal risks.
- **Delays in legalizations & apostilles:** Inaccurate translations may be rejected by authorities, causing costly delays.
- **Compliance and liability issues:** Non-compliant translations can lead to regulatory penalties or failed transactions.

## The Role of Sworn and Certified Legal Translators

In Belgium, sworn translations are often legally required for documents submitted to courts, notaries, or government agencies. Certified legal translators ensure that every document meets the highest standards. Their expertise includes:

- **Understanding legal terminology:** Both in source and target languages.
- **Ensuring confidentiality:** Legal translations often contain sensitive information; discretion is paramount.
- **Maintaining compliance:** Only certified legal translators can provide translations accepted by Belgian authorities.
