## Why Legal Translations Must Get It Right Every Time

Recent events at Brussels Airport have highlighted the complex interplay between international regulations, official documents, and cross-border legal compliance. For law firms, corporate legal teams, and public institutions in Belgium, the incident underscores a critical truth: legal translations are not just about words—they are about safeguarding trust, reliability, and compliance in high-stakes situations.

### The Stakes: Why Accuracy and Compliance Matter

Legal documents—whether international contracts, notarial deeds, or official documents—carry significant legal weight. A single misinterpretation or mistranslation can lead to:

- **Legal disputes**: Ambiguities or errors in multilingual communication can derail negotiations or render contracts void.
- **Regulatory non-compliance**: Inaccurate translations may fail to meet Belgian or EU requirements, resulting in costly delays or penalties.
- **Confidentiality breaches**: Sensitive information mishandled in the translation process can compromise cases, clients, or entire organizations.

At LegalTranslations, we prioritize **quality over speed** to ensure that every translation upholds the highest standards of accuracy, compliance, and confidentiality.

### Sworn Translations and Certified Legal Translators: The Gold Standard

Certain legal documents, such as those required for court proceedings, immigration, or notarization, demand **sworn translations**. Sworn translators—certified by Belgian authorities—guarantee that the translated text is legally valid and faithful to the original. This is essential for:

- **Legalizations and apostilles**: Ensuring documents are recognized internationally.
- **International contracts**: Preventing costly misinterpretations in cross-border agreements.
- **Official submissions**: Meeting the stringent requirements of Belgian public institutions and government agencies.

Our network of **certified legal translators** brings deep expertise in Belgian law and international legal terminology, offering **tailor-made solutions** for each client.

### Risks of Mistranslation: Lessons from Recent Incidents

The incident at Brussels Airport is a reminder of what can go wrong when translations are rushed or handled by non-specialists. Misinterpretations in international contracts, incomplete notarial deeds, or errors in compliance documents can lead to:

- **Litigation**: Legal challenges due to ambiguous contract terms.
- **Delays**: Hold-ups in regulatory approvals or immigration processes.
- **Financial loss**: Invalidated agreements or penalties due to non-compliance.

In legal matters, there is no room for error. That is why **proofreading and review**, rigorous **error correction**, and a **personal approach** are at the heart of our translation strategy.
