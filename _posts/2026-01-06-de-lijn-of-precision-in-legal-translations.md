Law firms, corporate legal departments, and public institutions in Belgium rely on sworn translations to ensure that contracts, official documents, and legalizations meet the highest standards of fidelity and legality.

**Key reasons accuracy and compliance are crucial:**

- **Legal Validity:** Only certified legal translators authorized in Belgium can provide translations that are recognized by Belgian courts and public authorities.
- **Risk Reduction:** Accurate translations prevent misunderstandings and misinterpretations in international contracts involving Belgian and international parties.
- **Data Security:** Confidentiality is paramount, as legal documents often contain sensitive information about Belgian businesses and individuals.

## The Role of Sworn and Certified Translators in Belgian Legal Translation Services

Sworn translations are performed by translators authorized by the Belgian courts, ensuring that your documents are legally valid and can be used in official proceedings throughout Belgium. Certified legal translators bring specialized knowledge of Belgian legal terminology and procedures, safeguarding against errors and omissions.

### Typical Documents Requiring Sworn or Certified Legal Translations in Belgium

- Notarial deeds  
- International contracts  
- Court judgments  
- Official documents for immigration or compliance  
- Legalizations & apostilles  

## Risks of Mistranslations in Legal Contexts Within Belgium

A minor error in a legal document can have major consequences. Mistranslations may compromise your position in Belgian courts, invalidate a contract, or expose your organization to legal risks. For public institutions and compliance officers in Belgium, incorrect translations can mean non-compliance with EU regulations or Belgian law.

**Common risks include:**

- **Contractual disputes** due to ambiguous clauses in international contracts involving Belgian entities  
- **Regulatory fines** from incorrect compliance documentation under Belgian law  
- **Loss of trust** with international partners due to inaccurate multilingual communication
